Altını Çizdiğim Yerler

  • ability to make choices in which the action and resulting outcome have not been determined by past events
  • Readiness Potential (RP), a build-up of electrical potential in the brain which manifests itself before a physical movement
  • RP leading up to a subject’s movement—which can be considered unconscious brain activity—began approximately half a second before the participant was aware of their own conscious intention to move.
  • the reality may be that the experience of free will is instead a post-event rationalisation leading to an artificial causal relation between intentions and actions.
  • free will as a function of three elements:

    • Priority. The thought must come before the action.

    • Consistency. The thought must be consistent with the action.

    • Exclusivity. The thought cannot be accompanied with other causes.

  • The fact that damage to the brain or chemical imbalances result in a loss of our ability to exercise our free will may suggest that our experience of free will itself is the result of unconscious neurobiological mechanisms which we can’t control.
